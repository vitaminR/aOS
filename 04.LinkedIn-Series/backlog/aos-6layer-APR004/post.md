## Copy/Paste (Codex-improved v2)

𝗠𝗼𝘀𝘁 𝗔𝗜 𝗮𝗿𝗰𝗵𝗶𝘁𝗲𝗰𝘁𝘂𝗿𝗲 𝗱𝗲𝗯𝗮𝘁𝗲𝘀 𝗮𝗿𝗲 𝘀𝘁𝗶𝗹𝗹 𝗷𝘂𝘀𝘁 𝗻𝗮𝗺𝗶𝗻𝗴 𝗽𝗿𝗼𝗯𝗹𝗲𝗺𝘀.

One platform says tool. Another says skill. Another says action, capability, workflow, agent, or subagent.

That confusion is survivable in a demo. In production, it turns into slower incident calls, blurry ownership, and teams debugging the wrong layer.

Today I'm publishing the first public version of the {a}OS 6-Layer AI/ML Model.

{a}OS-6L is a vendor-neutral reference model for describing, building, and troubleshooting AI systems with shared vocabulary and clean architectural boundaries.

It is built to make three things easier:

• 𝗻𝗮𝗺𝗲 the part of the system you are actually discussing  
• 𝗶𝘀𝗼𝗹𝗮𝘁𝗲 where the failure actually lives  
• 𝗿𝗼𝘂𝘁𝗲 the problem to the right expert faster  

The six layers are:

• 𝗟𝟲 𝗛𝘂𝗺𝗮𝗻 𝗜𝗻𝘁𝗲𝗻𝘁, 𝗣𝗼𝗹𝗶𝗰𝘆 & 𝗢𝘃𝗲𝗿𝘀𝗶𝗴𝗵𝘁  
• 𝗟𝟱 𝗢𝗿𝗰𝗵𝗲𝘀𝘁𝗿𝗮𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻, 𝗪𝗼𝗿𝗸𝗳𝗹𝗼𝘄𝘀 & 𝗥𝗼𝘂𝘁𝗶𝗻𝗴  
• 𝗟𝟰 𝗔𝗴𝗲𝗻𝘁 𝗟𝗼𝗴𝗶𝗰, 𝗦𝗸𝗶𝗹𝗹𝘀 & 𝗘𝘅𝗲𝗰𝘂𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻 𝗥𝗲𝗮𝘀𝗼𝗻𝗶𝗻𝗴  
• 𝗟𝟯 𝗧𝗼𝗼𝗹𝘀, 𝗜𝗻𝘁𝗲𝗿𝗳𝗮𝗰𝗲𝘀 & 𝗘𝘅𝘁𝗲𝗿𝗻𝗮𝗹 𝗔𝗰𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻𝘀  
• 𝗟𝟮 𝗖𝗼𝗻𝘁𝗲𝘅𝘁, 𝗠𝗲𝗺𝗼𝗿𝘆 & 𝗦𝗲𝗺𝗮𝗻𝘁𝗶𝗰 𝗦𝘁𝗮𝘁𝗲  
• 𝗟𝟭 𝗠𝗼𝗱𝗲𝗹𝘀, 𝗗𝗮𝘁𝗮 & 𝗖𝗼𝗺𝗽𝘂𝘁𝗲 𝗙𝗼𝘂𝗻𝗱𝗮𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻

The goal is not to invent more taxonomy for its own sake.

The goal is to give teams a practical operating language that still works when the stack spans Azure AI Foundry, AWS, Vertex, OpenAI, Anthropic, Palantir, or an internal enterprise platform glued together by caffeine and policy exceptions.

If a team can say, "This is a Layer 3 problem, not a Layer 1 problem," and immediately change who owns the fix, the model is doing real work.

Version 0.1 is the start.

Next up: canonical glossary, vendor crosswalks, rollout guidance, and reference patterns.
