# AFS01 — Visual Direction: "Your AI Platform Doesn't Ship What You Think It Ships"

## Plot Options

### Plot A — Layer Coverage Heatmap (Vertical Stack)

6 horizontal bars stacked vertically (L1 at bottom, L6 at top). Each bar is a gradient from green (strong) to red (absent):

- L1: Full green (strong)
- L2: Yellow-green (partial)
- L3: Green (good)
- L4: Green (good)
- L5: Orange-red (weak/preview)
- L6: Red (absent)

Label: "What Your Managed AI Platform Actually Covers"
Clean, dark background, no vendor logos.

**Pros:** Clear visual metaphor. Immediately communicates the gap without reading.
**Cons:** May look too simple for LinkedIn engagement.

### Plot B — The Gap Diagram (Recommended)

Split visual: left side shows a polished platform marketing image (clean, complete, glowing). Right side shows the same stack with the top two layers (L5, L6) visually cracked, missing, or under construction with scaffolding.

Label left: "What you're sold"
Label right: "What you ship"

**Pros:** Visceral contrast. High engagement potential. Tells the story in one image.
**Cons:** Requires more art direction.

### Plot C — The Iceberg

Classic iceberg diagram. Above water: "Model Hosting, Agent Framework, Tool Catalog" (what the platform ships). Below water: "Governance, Cost Enforcement, Network Isolation, Observability, Audit Trail, Compliance Reporting" (what you must build).

**Pros:** Familiar metaphor. Easy to understand.
**Cons:** Iceberg diagrams are overused on LinkedIn. May feel generic.

## Recommended

**Plot B** — The contrast between "what you're sold" vs "what you ship" creates the strongest engagement hook. It matches the post's contrarian thesis and avoids the overused iceberg format.

## Art Direction Notes

- No vendor logos or platform names in the image
- Dark or neutral background (professional, not flashy)
- Sans-serif typography
- The visual must work at LinkedIn mobile size (1200x627 or 1080x1080)
- The gap/crack visual should feel structural, not broken — this is an architecture observation, not an attack
